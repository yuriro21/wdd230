Visual Hierarchy

NVIDIA
nvidia.com

Visual hierarchy is the order in which the human eye perceives what it sees. Certain parts of the website are more important than others and we want those to get more attention than the less important parts. Here the first thing that we can see is a big image of a Graphic card, then a title, and next a button with shop now, those are according with the objective that is sell Graphic cards.

Hick's Law

Amazon
amazon.com

Hick’s Law says that every additional choice increases the time required to take a decision. We need to eliminate choices. To make a better web design, focus on eliminating distracting options throughout the design process. In an era of infinite choice, people need better filters! If you sell a huge number of products, add better filters for easier decision-making. On the left side, we can see this tool.

Fitt's Law

Amazon
amazon.com

Fitt’s law stipulates that the time required to move to a target area (e.g. click a button) is a function of the distance to the target and the size of the target. In other words, the bigger an object and the closer it is, the easier it is to use. In this image, we can see that what stands out the most are the purchase buttons, according to this law.
